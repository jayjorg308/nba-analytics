# Free-throw metrics use endpoint-parity semantics

The three league-baselined free-throw metrics — **FTA rate** (free-throw attempts per field-goal attempt), **free-throw conversion** (FT%), and **FT points share** — compare identical quantities on both sides, at the semantics the league endpoint can actually deliver:

- **Technicals are included, both sides, in all three.** League totals cannot exclude technical free throws (that would require a league-wide play-by-play corpus), so a hero-side exclusion would compare unlike quantities — the one dishonesty the exactness discipline forbids. The cost is small and known (technicals are ~1.6% of league FTA; hero-side 0 / 10 / 24), and the payload's technical count keeps the hero's clean cut computable.
- **The FGA denominator is pre-drop season FGA** (`totalShots + zoneConflictsDropped`): external totals know nothing of our ADR-0019 drops — the ADR-0030 precedent, applied again.
- **The both-cuts guard discipline:** a verdict claim on generation or conversion must hold on the hero's with-technicals *and* without-technicals cuts against the league number — the worst-case-bounds pattern (ADR-0037) applied to a semantic wrinkle instead of missing data. A claim that flips on 24 technical free throws was never sturdy enough to author.

**The trip taxonomy stays hero-descriptive** (ADR-0038's stance, verbatim): counts, tiers, and per-class conversion may ground absolute claims ("most of his trips are shooting fouls") but never comparative ones ("more bonus trips than league") — a league trip taxonomy would require reconstructing every league game, and that corpus does not exist. **Expected points per trip** (two or three free throws × conversion) is computed by the free-throw aggregation from its own league baseline, self-contained; at league conversion a two-shot trip prices at 1.566 points — above every zone on the floor — which is the number that lets copy weigh a drawn foul against a taken shot in the product's one value unit (PPS, never eFG% or TS%: ADR-0001 extended to the line).

**Shared constants, no new tunables:** the small-sample † flag is `SMALL_SAMPLE_MAKING_ATTEMPTS` (<50) counted in **free-throw attempts**, and the chart's dot floor is `ZONE_INCLUSION_MIN_ATTEMPTS` (15), also in free-throw attempts — the quantity being estimated is per-free-throw conversion, so the sample is free throws, not trips. Consequence on real data: Keyonte's three-shot class (11 trips, 33 FTA) clears the dot floor and draws; Cody's and-one conversion (10 FTA) falls under it and draws league-only. † means one thing everywhere on the page (ADR-0031), and "enough attempts to state" keeps one product-wide meaning.

**Consequences:** `aggregateFreeThrowMetrics` is a fourth pure function with a single production call site (ADR-0009/0011 extended to a fourth sibling domain), consuming the free-throw payload alone — the and-one/shot join is validated in Python at derive (ADR-0053) and no UI or aggregation re-performs it. A future contributor must not "clean up" the technicals-included semantics by excluding them on one side only, must not introduce an FT-specific sample constant, and must not convert a descriptive taxonomy statement into a league comparison because the number feels obviously high.
