# The portrait banner is a landing screen with guaranteed ink

On portrait handhelds the hero banner is `100svh` — the poster owns exactly the first viewport and the argument starts one scroll below (landscape keeps the 3:4 poster, where a viewport-height crop leaves no room for the title). Banner ink readability is guaranteed by controlling the ground, never by art direction: the narrow overlay's bottom grade runs to 0.85 black (a true title zone under the type), every line of banner ink carries an inherited glyph halo (`text-shadow` on the overlay), the "↓ the verdict" cue pins to the banner's bottom edge as a scroll affordance, and the thesis' name pair renders on one line in both layouts (`max-width: 18ch` + a measured `min(…, 10.6vw)` font guard).

**Why:** white ink over an uncontrolled B&W photo can never be *guaranteed* legible — the pre-grade scrim capped at 0.55 alpha and the jersey number ghosted through it behind the cue. Guarantees come from controlling a layer: a deep bottom grade is poster practice (photo above the fade keeps the drama), and the halo protects exactly where glyphs are for whatever the grade doesn't reach. Rejected: a solid plinth (stops being text-over-photo), and relying on per-hero focal-point cropping as the strategy (it silently breaks on every hero swap). `svh` over `vh`/`dvh` because it is the viewport with the browser's URL bar showing — the poster fits the first screen exactly on initial load.

**Consequences:** hero swaps need no ink audit of the new photo. `text-wrap: balance` only preserves the name pairing ("Is Cody Williams / taking good shots?") while BOTH halves fit the padded column — the vw font guard's coefficient is derived from the measured width of the longer half (~7.95× the font size; math recorded at `.hero-title` in `src/App.css`), so a new hero's longer thesis means re-deriving it. The optional per-hero team mark (`teamLogoPath`, ghosted into the wide layout's left column) is decorative only and hides on the narrow poster.
