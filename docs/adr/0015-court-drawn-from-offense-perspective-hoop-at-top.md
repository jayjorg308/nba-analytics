# The court is drawn from the offense's perspective — hoop at the top

The half court renders with the baseline and hoop at the TOP of the frame and the half-court line at the bottom, and `statsToSvg` (`src/chart/geometry.ts`) is a pure translation on both axes — no flips. This replaced the original hoop-at-bottom orientation, which shipped with a mirrored court.

**Why:** the NBA's `shotchartdetail` naming is the *offense's* perspective — negative `locX` is `Left Side(L)`, the shooter's left (verified on the launch payload: every Left Corner 3 has `locX` ∈ [−248, −222], every Right Corner 3 [221, 246]). Hoop-at-top is the one orientation where that convention maps to the image with no axis manipulation: the shooter faces up-frame, so shooter-left = image-left. The original implementation flipped y alone to get the familiar hoop-at-bottom tutorial view — but a single-axis flip is a *mirror*, not a change of viewpoint (a true hoop-at-bottom view flips x too), so left-corner shots rendered in what a basketball-literate reader sees as the right corner. Hoop-at-top also matches how reference sites (e.g. Basketball Reference) present shot charts, making side-by-side sanity checks direct.

**Consequences:** both axes of `statsToSvg` stay plain translations — any future orientation change must flip x and y *together* or not at all; a handedness guard in `src/chart/geometry.test.ts` ("hoop at top, shooter-left on image-left") fails on any single-axis flip. Zone label anchors and arc sweep flags are tuned for this orientation. Data is untouched: dots are the API's coordinates and zone assignment stays with the data (ADR-0012) — the mirror was purely presentational, which is why zone *labels* were always correct even while dot *positions* read backwards.
