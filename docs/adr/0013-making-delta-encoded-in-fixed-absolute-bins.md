# Making delta is encoded in fixed, absolute bins

The Zones view maps `makingDelta` to fill through a **binned** diverging scale (`src/chart/makingScale.ts`): a neutral gray band at ±2.5 pp around zero, two symmetric arms in equal 7.5 pp steps (edges at ±10 and ±17.5 pp), outer bins open-ended. The edges are fixed absolute percentage-point values (`MAKING_BIN_EDGES_PP`), not derived from any player's data. A `null` delta (zero-attempt zone) renders as **no paint**, never as neutral gray.

**Why:** a continuous gradient was rejected because it encodes false precision — per-zone making deltas ride on samples as small as ~34 attempts, and a smooth ramp invites reading +6.2 vs +7.9 as a real difference; bins say only what the data supports ("solidly above league"), and their exact edges can be annotated on the legend. Data-driven breaks (quantiles, extent scaling) were rejected because the engine is player-agnostic (the hero is a parameter): with fixed edges the same color means the same delta for every hero, season, and screenshot, whereas extent-scaled colors would repaint the entire court whenever one zone's delta moved and make two players' charts incomparable. The neutral band exists so near-league noise reads as "at league average" instead of painting a direction onto ±1 pp. No-paint-for-no-data is a semantic distinction: neutral gray asserts "at league average," which a zero-attempt zone cannot claim.

**Consequences:** the scale is a presentation *mapping* over the existing `ShotMetrics.zones[].makingDelta` — exactly the case ADR-0011 blesses; no quantity is computed and no rounding happens in the scale (bins compare raw floats; display rounding stays in `src/format.ts` — accepted edge: a −2.54 pp delta displays as "−2.5" but bins cold-1, because display and binning consume the raw float independently). `MAKING_BIN_EDGES_PP` is the single tunable; the legend derives its edge labels from it. Small-sample zones keep full color and carry the † flag (ADR-0008) — muting their fill would corrupt the magnitude encoding.
