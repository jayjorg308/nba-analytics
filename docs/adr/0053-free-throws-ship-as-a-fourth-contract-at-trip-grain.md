# Free throws ship as a fourth contract at trip grain

v2.6 adds the **free throw payload** as a fourth sibling contract (`public/data/<slug>/<season>.freethrow.json`), derived from the Case 3 corpus the repo already commits raw (PlayByPlayV3 + BoxScoreTraditionalV3 — zero new game pulls). Its unit is the **trip**: the free throws awarded from a single non-technical foul, shot as one visit to the line. The payload persists per-trip rows — `{gameId, period, clock, class, ftm, fta, shotId (and-one only)}`, normalized facts only (ADR-0048) — plus the technical free-throw count in `_meta` and the league free-throw baseline riding inside (the `zoneBaseline` pattern; summed from the season-totals artifact per ADR-0004). Own schema version, own golden, metric-free, **required for every registered hero** (one class of hero page, the ADR-0030 rule).

**The taxonomy is seven classes in two tiers**, and the tiers are the load-bearing structure: **attempt-equivalent trips** (shooting-foul 2 FT, shooting-foul 3 FT, bonus) end the possession in place of a field-goal attempt; **add-on trips** (and-one, flagrant, away-from-play, transition take, clear path) add points while the counted attempt or retained possession stands. A shooting-foul trip is a field-goal attempt the scorer never recorded — its free-throw count (2 vs 3) is the only surviving record of the denied attempt's point class, and no zone or coordinates exist for it (the FTA/POSS_END_FT shot-chart context measures are rejected by the current stats API; verified 2026-07-21). Technical free throws are never trips: real points, excluded from evaluation, counted and reported (the backcourt pattern).

**Why a fourth sibling, not an extension or a rollup:** the shot-context contract is *total over the shot payload* — exactly one row per post-drop shot (ADR-0039) — and most trips have no shot, so wedging them in dissolves the invariant that makes its coverage arithmetic mechanical. Same source, different universe: the ADR-0032 definition of a sibling. Pre-aggregated taxonomy counts were rejected because the payload keeps the finest meaningful grain and product grain is an aggregation concern (ADR-0030) — retuning how rare classes roll up for display must never be a schema bump. Trip grain is also what keeps the likely destination open: the eventual scoring-attempt model (Option B of the 2026-07-21 research) becomes a new pure aggregation plus a headline design decision — tier sums and and-one zone attribution read from rows that already exist. A design for that model that requires reshaping this payload is wrong about *that* design, not this contract.

**Derive-time oracle battery (hard-fail, the ADR-0046 ethos — a payload contradicting its oracles is never persisted):**

1. **Taxonomy totality** — every hero free-throw event classifies into exactly one trip class or technical, or the derive fails. Unlike assist evidence, where ambiguity is a real source condition, an unclassifiable free throw here means grammar drift and must be loud. The trip grammar (MISS-prefix make detection, same-clock trip grouping, causing-foul linkage skipping non-possession fouls) is versioned, the assist parser's discipline (ADR-0041).
2. **Per-game oracle** — Σ trip FTM/FTA + technicals equals the hero's `BoxScoreTraditionalV3` free-throw line in every game.
3. **And-one linkage** — every and-one trip's shot identity exists in the sibling shot payload as a made shot; exact join, never nearest-event (ADR-0036).

**No estimator, ever.** The 0.44 trip coefficient (and any successor) is permanently forbidden: hero-side trip counts are exact, and the moment an estimate enters, every exact claim on the page is contaminated by association. Where exact league-side data does not exist (league trip counts), the product goes descriptive (ADR-0038), never approximate.

**Proven on real data (2026-07-21 research, scratchpad reconstruction):** all 1,111 hero free-throw events across the 146-game corpus classified with zero unresolved; per-game FTM/FTA reconciliation exact for all three heroes in every game; season totals exact against the league endpoint (Cody 84/119, Keyonte 337/378, Shai 540/614). The stakes measured: FT points are 14.3% / 26.4% / 25.5% of the three heroes' scoring (league 15.9%), and non-and-one shooting fouls hide +8.8% / +15.2% / +16.1% more attempts than the shot payload can see.

**Consequences:** `hero:sync` copies four files per hero and a partial sync fails; the deployed-pair real-data guards extend to the fourth payload; `golden:regen` maintains a third golden pair; free-throw vocabulary enters the verdict lexicon's unshipped list in the contract PR and graduates only when the act ships (ADR-0029 mechanism, third use). A future contributor must not decompose trips into free-throw event rows (ordering noise below the semantic unit), must not fold trips into the shot-context contract, and must not guess a zone for a shooting-foul trip — the point class is knowable, the location is not (ADR-0012/0019 ethos).
