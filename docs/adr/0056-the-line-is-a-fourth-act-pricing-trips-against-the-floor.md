# THE LINE is a fourth act whose chart prices trips against the floor

The free-throw evidence renders as the hero page's fourth act — **`04 · THE LINE`**, after THE CREDIT, in the shared act recipe (ADR-0051: full-width section header, numbered kicker, visual left, data twin right). Placing it inside the SHOT CREATION act was rejected on ADR-0042's own logic: trips are a different measurement over a different universe (most trips have no shot), and a different measurement does not share a surface. Reordering the shipped acts was rejected as disturbance without argumentative gain. The kicker names a cut and a place, THE WHERE's flavor; the act description carries the framing sentence (the points his fouls created, on attempts the shot chart never counted) — structural copy, never per-hero.

**The chart encodes value, not generation.** The act has two stories: generation (FTA rate, FT points share — two numbers) and value (a trip outprices every zone on the floor — a genuinely spatial claim). Prominence follows differentiation (ADR-0018/0031): two-number stories need no chart, so generation leads the act description and the table twin, and the visual is the **line-vs-floor chart** — the creation act's dumbbell grammar reused on the points-per-attempt axis. One row per value-bearing trip class (two-shot, three-shot): his expected points per trip at his conversion vs the league's at league conversion — with the league zone-baseline PPS values drawn as **labeled reference ticks on the shared axis** (Restricted Area 1.34, Mid-Range 0.83 at minimum). The picture argues in one glance: a trip to the line is worth more than a shot from anywhere, and here is whether he cashes it. The cross-payload juxtaposition is formatting, not computation: zone PPS comes from `aggregateShotMetrics`, trip values from `aggregateFreeThrowMetrics`, and the UI computes no quantity (ADR-0011); claims spanning both surfaces are guarded across both metrics objects, as existing cross-payload guards already do. The dot floor and † follow ADR-0055 (counted in FTA, shared constants); a class under the floor draws the league dot only (no data is not a value claim — ADR-0013's rule, third use).

**No fifth headline card, and no verdict obligation.** The ADR-0031 posture, re-applied: the act is evidence; the two-axis decomposition (ADR-0016) is untouched; a hero's verdict *may* grow a guarded line-sentence backed by a new free-throw claim kind (for a 26%-at-the-line hero it almost certainly should), but nothing forces one. Free-throw vocabulary ('free throw', 'the line', 'trip', 'and-one', 'foul') sits in the verdict lexicon's unshipped list from the contract PR and graduates to the backed list only in the copy PR that ships this act — the ADR-0029 two-tier mechanism's third use. The table twin is the accessible data twin and the home of the taxonomy: tier-grouped trip classes with trips, FTM/FTA, and †-flagged per-class conversion under the season line (FTA rate, FT points share, free-throw conversion, each vs league) — FTA always visible as the honesty anchor, nothing hover-only (ADR-0027).

**The ADR-0051 amendment is intentional and deferred to the act's own PR.** ADR-0051's kickers currently name "the act's cut of the same reconciled season of *shots*"; THE LINE is a cut of the season's *scoring* that the shot record excludes, and the wording must be amended deliberately (cuts of the same reconciled season of scoring) — appended to ADR-0051 in the UI PR, when the fourth act exists, rather than quietly stretched now. Calling trips a cut of the shots would be exactly the small lie the docs culture exists to prevent.

**Consequences:** the act lands via `.section-caption` / `.section-layout` / `.table-panel` with at most a `--section-columns` knob (ADR-0051's extension path), so it cannot drift stylistically. A future contributor must not chart the trip taxonomy's composition (the diet-cut mistake ADR-0031 already corrected once), must not color the chart with the making palette (ADR-0013's unit contract), and must not move generation into the chart because the numbers are dramatic — dramatic numbers are what the act description and the verdict's guarded sentence are for.
