# The directory is a headshot marquee under the Good Shots wordmark

The hero index is redesigned from a uniform grid of poster tiles to a **headshot marquee**: the first registered hero as a full-width editorial feature (registry order is the directory order, so first place is the cover story), the remaining heroes as a name-only rail beneath an "Also on file" heading, every figure the player's **standard NBA headshot** — a transparent-background PNG cutout on a controlled dark ground — with the player's *name* as the tile copy. The site simultaneously gains its first persistent chrome: a **site navbar** carrying the wordmark **Good Shots** (the site's name), fixed over every page's top edge, transparent with halo-guaranteed ink over the poster banner and gaining a blurred solid ground once scrolled. The wordmark is a plain anchor to the index and is the bar's only item.

**Why the redesign:** the old grid repeated the identical thesis question ("Is ___ taking good shots?") in poster type on every tile — when every tile is a poster, none is, and the one differentiating fact (who) sat in an 11px kicker that wrapped badly. The grid shape also assumed a roster the August launch will not have: at one or two registered heroes, `auto-fill` tiles read as an empty shelf, while the marquee-plus-rail form is *designed* for a small roster (one hero = the marquee alone, and it reads as intentional). Decided by a four-variant prototype on the real registry data (marquee/action-shots, ledger rows, name-led poster wall, marquee/headshots) judged at roster sizes 4, 2, and 1; the headshot marquee won.

**Why headshots on the directory and action posters on the hero page:** the two surfaces answer different questions. The directory answers *who is on file* — faces answer it instantly, in uniform framing, with zero per-hero art direction (no focal points, no crop tuning, no sourcing: the asset is a formula, `cdn.nba.com/headshots/nba/latest/1040x760/<playerId>.png`, with `playerId` already in the shot payload's `_meta`). The hero page answers *the thesis* — where the editorial action poster argues literally (ADR-0021) and stays untouched. The split also makes the surfaces read as deliberately different rather than the directory being a shrunken hero page. The cutout-on-dark-ground treatment keeps ink guarantees trivial (no photo behind text, so no scrim needed — the ground is controlled), and the product's B&W identity applies to headshots exactly as to posters.

**The navbar is navigation, not a switcher.** ADR-0022's consequence — cross-hero navigation is "the index tile and the quiet footer link" — is amended: the wordmark is now the persistent way home on every page (the old mobile reality was that the footer link sat eight screens down, so a shared hero link had no practical route to the directory). Everything ADR-0018/0022 actually protect stands: the bar contains no hero list, no dropdown, no view state — plain anchors between complete pages, and the hero page's quiet "All players" footer link remains. The bar is `fixed` (never in flow), so the portrait banner still owns exactly the first viewport (ADR-0025); its over-poster state guarantees ink by the banner's own halo recipe, since the photo's top edge carries no grade. The wordmark is set in the sans face: ADR-0020 restricts the display face to poster scale, and a navbar wordmark is not poster scale — a display-face brand mark, if branding ever wants one, is a deliberate future amendment (or an image asset).

**Contract consequences:** `HeroBannerConfig` gains a required `headshotPath` (conventional `img/<slug>-headshot.png`, committed under `public/`). The authoring tripwire (ADR-0063) checks the asset's existence exactly as it does the banner's — a registered hero without a committed headshot is a red suite — and the scaffolder emits the conventional path as a mechanical field with the download formula in a comment (structure, never judgment: a headshot needs no authoring). The directory's meta eyebrow (team · number · season, marquee only) is **derived** from the authored kicker by dropping its player-name segment (`indexMetaOf`, `src/heroes/types.ts`) rather than authored twice; the index test holds every registered hero's derivation non-empty, name-free, and season-bearing, so a kicker that breaks the "Name · Team · Nº N · Season" shape fails a test instead of shipping a broken eyebrow. Headshot `img` elements are decorative (`alt=""`) — the adjacent name is the accessible text — and headshot provenance is recorded in `docs/image-credits.md`.

**Page structure and copy:** the index's `h1` is the site name, **visually hidden** (`.sr-only`) — the navbar wordmark already renders it, and setting it visibly at poster scale would compete with the marquee's player name, which is the page's visual headline. The rail's heading is **"More verdicts"**: the product's own payoff word, established by the marquee's cue directly above it, rather than a roster label. That framing is deliberate — the rail offers more of the same argument, where the first draft's "Also on file" (an echo of a working title the wordmark no longer carries) made those players sound like a leftovers shelf. The heading names its section through `aria-labelledby`, the pattern the acts already use, so the accessible name cannot drift from the visible words; the first draft paired an `aria-label` of "More players" with a visible "Also on file", which is precisely that drift.

**Amendment (2026-07-24, same day — responsive closures from device-size review):** three fixes, two of them with root causes worth recording.

- **The portrait marquee is the banner's poster, not a stack.** The figure fills the panel *grounded on its bottom edge* with the text over its lower stretch on a title-zone grade plus glyph halo — ADR-0025's recipe, reused. The first attempt lifted the figure above the text and faded its bottom edge; that hid the cut but not the float, and floating was the actual complaint. Nothing hovers if nothing is lifted. Sizing is `contain` in a box oversized past the panel width (`cover` scales a landscape source to the portrait panel's *height* and blows it up into a face close-up; plain `contain` letterboxes it into a third of the panel). The measured source geometry is the license for grounding it: the NBA headshot PNG is 1040×760 with ~5% transparent margins per side and **0% at the bottom** — the figure is already flush at the image's own bottom edge, so it lands flush on the panel's. Measure the asset before designing the crop; the earlier "wide transparent margins" comment on the rail cards was a guess, and it was wrong.
- **Never `aspect-ratio` + `max-height` on a full-bleed element.** The hero banner's landscape-handheld state capped height that way, and when the cap binds, the constraint transfers *back through the ratio to the box's width* — the banner silently stopped spanning the shell, leaving a ~13% dead strip at the right edge on a 932×430 phone (invisible at desktop and in a preview pane that never gets that short). It now computes height directly: `aspect-ratio: auto; height: min(100svh, calc(100vw * 600 / 1126))`. A cap on one axis of a ratio-sized box is never only a cap on that axis.
- The stacked marquee form is **portrait-only** (landscape handhelds keep the side-by-side form — the width-only breakpoint had given landscape phones a tiny centered figure in a void), and the hero banner's landscape state gains a paired tune: the photo's mask fades in later and the left scrim holds longer, because the 60% panel × 72px title cap pair (ADR-0021) was tuned at desktop width and the title's tail reached the player's white jersey at phone-landscape widths. The navbar also gains a soft top scrim (a pseudo-element that fades out as the scrolled ground fades in): both surfaces behind it are ungraded at the top edge, since the banner's grade is bottom-anchored and the portrait figure now reaches the panel's top.

**What must not regress:** do not reintroduce the thesis question at tile scale (the name is the tile copy; the question is the hero page's h1); do not add hero links, menus, or view state to the navbar (a hamburger or player dropdown is the switcher ADR-0018 forbids — if the roster ever outgrows the rail, that is a directory-layout problem, not a navbar problem); do not put the navbar in flow above the banner (ADR-0025's first-viewport guarantee); do not swap the hero page's poster for the headshot; and do not hand-author the meta eyebrow into hero copy — fix the kicker or the derivation, not the symptom.
