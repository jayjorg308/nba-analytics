# Growth is vs-league residual movement, argued in a gated coda on the canonical page

Season-over-season growth is measured as **movement in the vs-league residuals** — selection Δ, making PPS Δ, per-zone diet-share gap and making Δ — each season against **its own season's league baseline**, never as movement in raw rates. It is argued in the **growth coda**: a closing SEASON OVER SEASON section on the canonical season's page, deliberately outside the numbered acts (the act kickers name cuts of one reconciled season — ADR-0051; the coda is the one thing on the page that is not). The coda renders iff the canonical season has a **prior argued season** — the hero's most recent earlier season argument, so both comparison sides passed all five gates by construction — and it appears on flip day with no additional maturity bar. Its scope is the two-axis spine plus the zone grain, and that visible scope bounds what a growth-sentence may claim. **Growth claims** are a new claim kind: assertions consume both seasons' deployed payloads and assert the movement itself. A growth-sentence is legal, never mandatory; only the canonical verdict may carry one, and the prior season's verdict is **frozen verbatim** at the flip — present tense and all; the season labels on every surface carry the temporal frame. Growth vocabulary enters the lexicon's unshipped list in the build PR and graduates when the first hero ships a growth-sentence.

**Why:** a raw cross-season comparison conflates player change with league drift (three-point inflation is the classic), and the product's entire grammar is already vs-league (ADR-0002/0016) — growth in the residuals is the only comparison that isolates the player. Prior *argued* seasons only: an unargued season's numbers would be evidence with no page home and no gate provenance — Cody's unargued 2024-25 never qualifies, however its data looks. No maturity bar: gate-pass is the bar (ADR-0059's stance — the gates define enough season to argue about), † flags and the frontier byline carry thin-sample honesty, and the corner-split precedent already covers a section that is present iff there is something to show. The movement needs its own claim kind because two static per-season claims can both hold while the movement claim fails as displayed (ADR-0023's rounding lesson); the delta itself must be asserted. Frozen verbatim because rewriting a guarded verdict for tense is claim-mapping churn for zero informational gain, and sports prose wears the historic present naturally.

**Consequences:** the canonical page fetches five payloads — its own four plus the prior season's shot payload only (the coda's scope needs nothing else); the prior season's full four still deploy because its own page requires them. A new pure growth aggregation computes every displayed movement (presentation never computes — ADR-0011; a displayed movement is the difference of the displayed anchors — ADR-0023). `hero:report` gains a GROWTH section with claim-headroom rows so a flip-day growth-sentence is authored report-first, and post-flip growth claims move daily under claim headroom and halt-and-rewrite like every live claim. The feature ships dark: no hero has two argued seasons until Ace's flip, so the coda and claim kind are proven by fixture-driven component and guard tests (the v3 pre-positioning pattern), and the flip PR is the first real instance. Creation, assist, and line growth are future scope extensions requiring their own decision — never silent additions to the coda or the claim kind.
