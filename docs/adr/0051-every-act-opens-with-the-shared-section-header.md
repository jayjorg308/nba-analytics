# Every act opens with the shared section header

ZONE BY ZONE's header was the zone table's caption: it sat inside the right-hand column, in a grid track shared with the court's toggle and legend, while SHOT CREATION and ASSISTED MAKES opened with full-width section headers. The page now has one act opener — every section renders a full-width header in the section title recipe above a shared split layout (visual left, data twin right, stacking below 1024px). One CSS recipe (`.section-caption` / `.section-layout` / `.table-panel`) serves all three acts, so a new section cannot drift; the drift between the first act and the later two is what forced this decision.

Consequences: the h2 no longer names the zone table (the section takes `aria-labelledby`; the table names itself via `aria-label`, as its siblings already did), and the court/table subgrid — whose only job was registering the caption and chart controls into one shared header track — is retired. The assisted plot's narrower column survives as a `--section-columns` custom-property knob on the shared layout, never a `grid-template-columns` override that would outrank the stacked breakpoint.

Alternating chart/table sides per act was considered and rejected: the page is one argument (ADR-0018/0031), and "visual left, numbers right" is a grammar the reader learns once.

Companion rule: desktop tables never scroll. In the split layout's tight band (~1025–1140px viewports) the zone table's nowrap row labels wrap instead of engaging the shared scroll wrapper — a scrollbar on exactly one table read as an inconsistency; `.zone-scroll` is for phones.

**Amendment (2026-07-20) — acts open with a numbered kicker.** Each act header carries an eyebrow line (`.section-kicker`, the hero banner kicker's type metrics in the caption's color system) naming the act's cut of the same reconciled season of shots: `01 · THE WHERE` (place), `02 · THE HOW` (manner of arrival — the creation table's own "How the shot arrived" vocabulary at act scale), `03 · THE CREDIT` (the scorer's ledger, in ADR-0049's own vocabulary; full third beat — ADR-0042 amendment). The verdict argues in this order (ADR-0018), so the kickers typeset the page's story spine. Kickers name the **cut, never the rhetoric**: earlier drafts used `THE WHY` (named the argument's function, not a dimension — a shot's creation context, "catch-and-shoot, 12 on the clock, defender 4–6 ft," is circumstance, not explanation) and `THE WHO` (promised authorship the credit ledger cannot attest — ADR-0049's exact forbidden inference, and no teammate is ever named in the act; the word stays in reserve for data that names passers). The argumentative payoff stays in each section description ("why his conversion lands where it does" — ADR-0031's "the why" is the description's job). Kicker copy is structural and direction-free — never a per-hero claim — so it carries no verdict-guard obligations and never changes on a hero swap.

**Amendment (2026-07-21) — the kickers name cuts of the season's scoring, not only its shots.** v2.6's fourth act (`04 · THE LINE`, ADR-0056) is not a cut of the shots: trips to the line are the season's scoring events the shot record excludes — most have no shot at all (the shooting-foul trip replaces an attempt the scorer never recorded, ADR-0053). The amendment above therefore reads "the act's cut of the same reconciled season of **scoring**": the first three acts cut the shots (place, manner, credit), the fourth cuts the scoring the shot chart cannot see (the line). This wording change was flagged the day the act was designed and deferred to the act's own PR deliberately (ADR-0056) — calling trips a cut of the shots would have been exactly the small lie the kicker discipline exists to prevent. Everything else above stands: cut never rhetoric, structural and direction-free, the payoff in the description.
