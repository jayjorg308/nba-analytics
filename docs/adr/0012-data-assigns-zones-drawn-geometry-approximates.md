# The data assigns zones; drawn geometry only approximates them

`SHOT_ZONE_BASIC` (with its area/range refinements) on each enriched shot is the sole authority for which zone a shot — and every number derived from it — belongs to. The court regions the Zones view draws (`zoneRegions()` in `src/chart/geometry.ts`) are presentation shapes that approximate those zones; where drawn geometry and the data's assignment disagree, **the data wins**. The UI must never reassign a shot's zone from its coordinates — not for shading, not for tooltips, not for counts.

**Why:** the zone boundary is not recoverable from coordinates alone. In the launch payload the Restricted Area's radial envelope tops out at ~39.66 stats units and In-The-Paint (Non-RA)'s begins at ~40.46 — the two sides of the same nominal 40-unit (4 ft) boundary, about an inch apart, and both rounding to 40. A shot logged at exactly 40 would be unclassifiable from `locX/locY`; the NBA's assignment, made from its own unrounded distance, is the only ground truth we have. A coordinate-based reassignment would also be a second implementation of zone logic living in the UI — exactly the class of silently-disagreeing duplicate ADR-0011 exists to prevent. The drawn regions exist so the reader can see *where* the zones are, never to *decide* what they are.

**Consequences:** the Zones view shades regions from `ShotMetrics.zones[]`, keyed by zone name, never from dot positions — so a dot may occasionally sit a hair on the "wrong" side of a drawn boundary, and that is correct behavior, not a bug to fix by nudging data. The geometric classifier that mirrors the painter's stack (`classifyByGeometry`) is a **TEST-ONLY** instrument for measuring drawn-vs-data agreement (509/509 on the launch payload, which happens to contain no boundary-sitting shots); it must never be imported by production code. If a future payload disagrees with the drawn regions, the test documents the disagreement and the region shapes may be tuned for fidelity — but the data's assignment stands.
