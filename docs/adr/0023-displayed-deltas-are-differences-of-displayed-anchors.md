# Displayed deltas are differences of displayed anchors

Wherever a delta renders **beside the two numbers it relates** — the headline pair's "his choices" and "his conversion", `hero:report`'s decomposition ladder and its zone-table Δ pp column — the delta is computed from the anchors' own display roundings (in integer display units — `formatSignedGap`, `src/format.ts`), never rounded from the raw delta. Raw floats stay the truth everywhere else: the aggregation returns them untouched (ADR-0007/0011), the verdict guards assert on them, and the report's anchors still print them at 3 decimals.

**Why:** three independently correct roundings need not agree with each other. George's making block read "1.07 he scored / 1.02 expected from his diet / +0.04 his conversion" — every number rounded correctly from raw, and the reader's subtraction says 0.05. A page that leads with a verdict cannot have numbers that fail grade-school arithmetic; the reader's own subtraction is the first credibility check the argument faces. Rounding the anchors and deriving the deltas is the one assignment under which every displayed identity holds simultaneously — and because anchor gaps telescope, the full ADR-0016 decomposition (league diet + selection Δ + making Δ = actual) holds at display grain with no extra machinery. Showing more decimals was rejected: independent rounding can disagree at any fixed precision, and 3dp clutter belongs in `hero:report`, not on the poster page. The accepted cost, stated plainly: a displayed gap can differ from the raw delta by one display unit when the anchors round in opposite directions (George shows +0.05 where the raw delta is +0.044; the guard bands in the verdict guards keep consuming the raw value, so verdict semantics are untouched).

**Consequences:** a delta shown **without** its anchors keeps rounding from the raw value — the app's zone-table Making Δ has no FG% columns beside it by design (ADR-0018), and the court labels likewise — so the same fact can differ by one display unit between surfaces; within any single surface the arithmetic always checks, and that is the invariant that matters. The committed guard (`src/app/HeadlineBanner.identity.test.tsx`) renders the headline pair for the golden fixture and every registered hero's deployed payload and asserts all four identities in integer cents — a hero swap or `hero:sync` cannot resurface the mismatch quietly. Making-scale binning still consumes raw deltas (the documented ADR-0013 display-vs-bin edge is unchanged). `formatSignedPps2` (round the raw delta) is deleted; when a new surface shows a delta beside its anchors, reach for `formatSignedGap` — never reintroduce the raw-delta form there.
