# Creation renders as a second act, families render whole, and no new scales ship

The creation section is the hero page's **second act**: it sits after the court and zone table, under the section-title recipe (`SHOT CREATION` + a one-line description stating the comparison class), mirroring the `hero-layout` pattern — a hand-rolled SVG **creation-diet chart** (paired horizontal bars per context: his attempt share vs the league's, the two families as stacked groups, neutral chart ink) beside a **creation table** in the zone table's column philosophy (`Context / FGA / Share / Lg share / PPS (lg)` — payoff first, reference trailing, FGA always visible as the honesty anchor). Every context of a shipped family always renders, and conversion carries the same `< 50 attempts` † flag constant as the rest of the page.

**Why second act, not third headline:** the verdict now argues on two levels — the two-axis thesis and the creation why (ADR-0029) — and the evidence unfolds in the same order the verdict argues: court + table back the thesis first, then the creation section backs the why. Wedging creation between the headline cards and the court would put second-topic evidence in front of the primary claim's, and would disturb a shipped, tuned layout for no argumentative gain. For the same reason there is **no third headline card and no creation-diet-weighted expected PPS scalar** in v2.0: creation is the why, not a third axis of the ADR-0016 decomposition — inventing a second decomposition before the data proves the need would be machinery for its own sake.

**Why no inclusion gate for contexts:** zones could be excluded from the mix view because the mix view is not a partition display; a context family **is** one — its contexts sum to the (attributed) season attempts, and dropping a low-volume context silently corrupts every displayed share and the league comparison with it. So the v1 discipline translates as: families render whole (muted-never-deleted, taken all the way), small samples are **flagged** on the conversion axis with the shared constant so † means one thing everywhere on the page, and a zero-attempt context shows its share and makes no PPS claim (ADR-0013's "no data ≠ at league average," ported).

**Why no new color scale:** the making palette means "FG% delta vs league" and nothing else — ADR-0013's fixed bins are what let the same color mean the same delta everywhere, and reusing the arms for a different unit (creation PPS deltas) would break exactly that. A creation-value encoding would need its own fixed-bin scale contract with its own guarded palette (the ADR-0014 pattern); v2.0 deliberately doesn't pay that until the panel proves it needs color at all. Likewise **no tooltips and no detail cards**: the table already carries the full story, nothing is hover-only (ADR-0027's lesson), and the bars are a presentation mapping over aggregation outputs (ADR-0011) with direct labels.

**Consequences:** `hero:report` grows a creation section (creation diet vs league, PPS by context, the unattributed count, flags) — the why-sentence is written from it, keeping the hero-swap recipe intact. When defender distance ships, it is a third group in the same chart and table, not a new surface. A future contributor must not add an FG%/eFG% column to the creation table (PPS is the unit — ADR-0001), must not color the bars by the making palette, and must not "complete the partition" by hiding small contexts.

**Amendment (2026-07-15) — the chart encodes value, not diet.** The section's original visual — paired bars of attempt share — led with the family's *least* new information: the diet cut largely restates the zone story (inside-10-ft share ≈ the rim-heavy diet the zone table already argued), and reading it as "the same thing again" was the correct reading. What no other surface can say is what each kind of shot is **worth**: creation-context PPS vs the league's. Prominence follows differentiation (ADR-0018), so the chart is now a **dumbbell per context** — his PPS dot vs the league's on one shared positional axis with honest tick labels (a non-zero baseline is legitimate for point comparisons; the making palette stays untouched — positional encoding needs no color scale). A zero-attempt context draws only the league dot: no data is not a value claim (ADR-0013). Diet shares move to the table, whose columns reorder payoff-first: `Context / FGA / PPS (lg) / Share / Lg share`. The section description now states its role plainly: *why his conversion lands where it does*. The launch hero is the argument for the change: his catch-and-shoot crater (0.71 vs 1.10) was a trailing reference digit; it is now the picture.

**Amendment (2026-07-15) — the General family renders two-tier.** The NBA's General taxonomy is not flat: the classifier only classifies jump shots outside 10 feet ('Catch and Shoot' = ≤2s hold, no dribble; 'Pull Ups' = 1+ dribbles), 'Less than 10 ft' is a location bucket with no creation signal, and 'Other' is the classifier's gap (10 ft+ attempts fitting neither — league-wide ~0.5%). Presented as four flat rows the family read as three creation categories plus an intruder, so it now renders at its true shape: **Inside 10 ft** vs the **jumper parent (10 ft and out — Catch and Shoot + Pull Ups + Other, summed on both sides per ADR-0004, the ADR-0016 combined-threes pattern)** over its three child rows, with a note stating plainly that tracking classifies no creation at the rim. The chart keeps parent and children in separate groups so no bar double-counts another. The rollup is aggregation-owned (`aggregateCreationMetrics`); the payload keeps the NBA's flat literals — no schema change. The parent row is also the payoff: it states the value of a hero's jump shooting as one sample-safe line (the launch hero: 227 jumpers at 0.749 PPS vs the league's 1.021).

**Amendment (2026-07-18) — the Other residual is table-only, and a charted player dot requires the zone-inclusion bar.** The 'Other' residual forced both calls: it is the classifier's gap, not a creation category — ~0.5% of attempts league-wide, 0–2 for every current hero — and a couple of lucky makes rendered as absurd dots (Shai's 1-of-1 two read 2.00 PPS, George's 2-of-2 threes read 3.00), stretching the shared axis by up to a full point of PPS and compressing every real dumbbell into a fraction of the plot. Binned color can absorb an outlier (ADR-0013's outer bins are open-ended); a shared positional axis cannot — one unplaceable value costs every other row its resolution. So, first: **the chart's jumper children are Catch and Shoot and Pull Ups only — the Other residual draws no chart row at all.** A residual is a coverage fact, not a kind of creation worth a dumbbell; its attempts remain inside the jumper parent's summed line, its full row (FGA, share, †-flagged PPS) stays in the table, and a standing note states the exclusion plainly. This does not puncture the partition this ADR protects: the shares and the parent sum are unchanged everywhere they are stated — the chart simply declines to give the classifier's gap a row. Second, for the contexts the chart does row, **the zero-attempt rule generalizes into a dot floor**: under `ZONE_INCLUSION_MIN_ATTEMPTS` (15 — deliberately the shared inclusion bar, not a new tunable, so "enough attempts to state" means one thing product-wide) a context draws only its league dot, because a handful of attempts is not a value claim the chart can place; the table keeps the flagged figures and the notes disclose any unplaced charted context. The † discipline is unchanged at or above the bar (15–49 attempts keep their dot and their flag).
