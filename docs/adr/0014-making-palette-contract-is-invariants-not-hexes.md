# The making palette's contract is invariants, not hexes

The seven `--making-*` fills are hand-picked **per theme** — light and dark each get steps selected against their own surface, never auto-flipped or auto-derived from the other mode — and what makes a palette *valid* is defined by a committed guard test, `src/chart/makingScale.contrast.test.ts`, not by the specific hex values. The guard parses the actual stylesheets (`src/App.css`, `src/index.css`) and asserts three invariants in both modes: (1) every fill ≥ 4.5:1 WCAG contrast against that mode's label ink (`--text-h`), (2) per-arm luminance strictly monotone, receding toward the surface at the neutral end (darker outward in light mode, lighter outward in dark), (3) a chromatically gray neutral midpoint.

**Why:** the hexes are expected to change — the dark set is under active tuning — but the invariants are what carry the encoding: monotone luminance is what makes "stronger color = bigger delta" legible, a hue at the diverging midpoint would read as data (ADR-0013's neutral means *no signal*), and a sub-4.5:1 fill would swallow the direct zone labels. Parsing the committed CSS means there is no test-side hex duplicate to drift out of sync with the stylesheet. Auto-deriving the dark palette from the light one was rejected because dark surfaces invert the recede direction (fills must *lighten* outward) and need their own chroma choices to hold label contrast.

**Consequences:** tune colors freely — a palette change is done when the guard passes, in both modes; never weaken the guard's assertions to admit a palette that fails them. One deviation from the dataviz method is deliberate and should not be "fixed": the palest steps sit below the advisory 2:1 fill-vs-surface floor because zone fills are large regions delineated by court-line strokes and carrying direct labels, not discrete marks that must be findable against the background. The WCAG math is intentionally local to the test file — the dataviz skill's validator lives at a machine-specific temp path and must never be imported by committed code.
