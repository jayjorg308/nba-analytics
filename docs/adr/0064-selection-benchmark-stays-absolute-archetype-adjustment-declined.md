# The selection benchmark stays absolute; archetype-adjusted selection is declined

We prototyped archetype-adjusted selection — the deferred ROADMAP "Beyond v3" item that ADR-0002 always flagged would "supersede ADR-0002 deliberately or not at all." The answer is **not at all**, for now: the selection axis stays benchmarked against the whole-league diet, and role is never folded into the selection number. ADR-0002 stands, reaffirmed by evidence rather than inertia.

**What we built (throwaway prototype, 2026-07-24).** A rules-based cohort benchmark. Cluster players on ROLE features that are upstream of shot location (usage rate, a height band, on-ball pull-up share) — never on the zone diet itself, which would be circular — then re-benchmark the hero's diet-weighted expected PPS against his cohort's diet instead of the league's, holding zone PPS at league (the value of a location is a league fact; only the benchmark *diet* changes). Self-checked against the tool: the cohort math reproduced each hero's league selection Δ exactly before we trusted any archetype number. The prototype was deleted; this record is its only durable output.

**What it does — three heroes, three directions:**

| player | role | league selection Δ | archetype Δ | movement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cody Williams (low-usage wing) | value-neutral diet | +0.007 | +0.011 | +0.003 |
| Shai Gilgeous-Alexander (high-usage creator) | mid-range-heavy | −0.054 | −0.005 | +0.049 |
| Daniel Gafford (rim-runner, non-hero control) | ~80% at the rim | +0.158 | +0.087 | −0.071 |

Archetype adjustment is a **role-normalization**: it pulls every player's selection toward "average for his archetype," stripping the part of the league comparison that is only role. It moves ~nothing for a value-neutral diet (Cody's rim-heavy, three-light diet is a value-neutral reshuffle — more high-value rim, fewer above-the-break threes, and they cancel in expected PPS, so no benchmark moves it), moves a role-*deflated* number UP (high-usage creators look below-average versus the league and normal versus their peers), and moves a role-*inflated* number DOWN (rim-runners look elite versus the league and average versus their peers). The mechanism is sound and does exactly what ADR-0002 named as the position-blind limitation. Refining the cohort matters (a coarse usage×height wing cohort put Cody at −0.012 by mixing in spot-up snipers; adding the on-ball axis to reach his true peers moved him back to +0.011), which is itself a warning: the archetype scalar is benchmark-fragile for a value-neutral diet, and a number that flips sign on cohort choice cannot anchor an authored verdict.

**Why we decline it anyway.** It moves the selection number most for precisely the players whose story the *other* axes already tell — and normalizing their selection deletes that story. SGA's honestly-negative selection is the hook of his whole page ("low-value shots, made anyway, plus a quarter of his scoring at the line"); that sentence works only because the number was not graded on a curve. Archetype "corrects" it to neutral and the hook is gone. Every current hero is either Cody (no movement) or a creator/mispriced-diet player whose sharp selection verdict archetype would soften — Keyonte George sits in SGA's cohort at 27.5% usage, and Ace Bailey's mid-range/long-two selection problem is the same shape. The deeper conflict is philosophical: the tool grades selection against an **absolute, role-blind floor** (ADR-0002) so that role-driven quirks surface as selection *facts*, then making and the line explain whether they matter. Archetype grades on a **curve**, absorbing those quirks into the benchmark — the opposite move, and it dissolves the selection / making / line separation the whole product is built on. An absolute selection standard is not a limitation to fix; it is what lets the two-axis decomposition (ADR-0016) and THE LINE (ADR-0056) do their work.

**Consequences and standing guardrail.** Do not fold role into the selection scalar, and do not grade selection on a curve; the comparison class stays the whole-league diet, stated plainly (ADR-0002). The one additive thing the prototype surfaced — the tool never tells the reader what is *typical for a role* — is a descriptive color gap, not a verdict gap: the tool answers an absolute question and names its comparison class, and making + the line already say whether the diet worked. If that gap is ever filled it belongs as role-relative diet-*shape* context, never as a re-graded selection number. Archetype-adjusted selection may return only for a hero the whole-league benchmark genuinely mis-prices (a rim-running big, a pure spot-up shooter) — none of the current four are — and then only as a clearly-labeled **secondary** lens with league as the authored default (the ADR-0026 toggle-as-view-state pattern), never replacing the absolute number a verdict is authored against. The league-scale role corpus and the derive-side analytical index the ROADMAP anticipated for this feature stay unbuilt until that case exists.
