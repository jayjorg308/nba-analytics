# A live season renders only after its gates pass, and the flip is a human copy event

A living season becomes the season a hero page renders only when **all five eligibility gates pass on the live data**, evaluated daily through the reconciled frontier (ADR-0058). Gate 2's mechanical reading for this trigger: every evaluation zone at or above `ZONE_INCLUSION_MIN_ATTEMPTS` (15) — the crisp form of what every registered hero-season has satisfied. Until that day the season loop **runs dark**: it pulls, derives, and reports daily, and publishes nothing. The **live flip** itself is a human copy event, never automated: the live verdict is authored from that morning's `hero:report`, the guard's claim mapping is written with it, and the season swap ships as a reviewed PR — exactly the existing hero-swap recipe, triggered by a gate-pass instead of a calendar. A hero with no prior argument (the rookie case) is *born live*: the page first exists the day the gates first pass. A hero with a shipped completed-season argument keeps it until their own flip PR replaces it — going live never silently overwrites an argued page.

**Why:** the alternative — rendering the live season from opening night behind an "early season" presentation state — fails two standing decisions at once. ADR-0003's rationale is that thin data makes the *worst* page (the more honestly we suppress noise, the emptier the chart), and opening night is maximal thinness: a page class designed specifically for the weeks the product is at its worst. And ADR-0030's one-class-of-hero-page rule exists so the page never forks into conditionals; a pre-gate state is a second class by definition. A calendar flip was rejected because it can land on a season still failing a gate. Gate-pass is the one trigger that is objective, loop-reportable, and already the meaning of hero eligibility — the gates were always the definition of "enough season to argue about"; on a living season they simply get evaluated repeatedly until they say yes. The flip stays human because verdicts are authored, never computed (ADR-0017) — the machinery's job is to say "the gates pass today; here is the report," not to write copy.

**Live verdict discipline — nothing loosens:** the deployed payload now moves daily, so an authored claim can invert mid-season. When it does, the guard fails, the morning's publish halts (ADR-0057), and the remedy is the one ADR-0017 always prescribed: rewrite the copy and the claim mapping together — a **halt-and-rewrite morning** is the system working. Two authoring aids ship instead of any guard relaxation: `hero:report` gains a **claim headroom** section (each guarded claim's distance from its threshold, so live verdicts are written with deliberate margin — an authoring input, never a guard input), and live pages carry structural through-date framing ("Through Jan 14 · 34 games", from the frontier metadata) so the verdict reads as a statement about the season so far — structural copy, identical treatment for every live hero, no verdict-guard obligations.

**Consequences:** the dark period's output is derived payloads and the daily report only — no sync, no deployed change, and a "gates now pass" line in the report is the flip's starting gun. A rookie's flip may land mid-winter and that is the design, not a delay: the page appears arguing from data that clears the same bars every completed-season hero cleared. A future contributor must not add a pre-gate page state or a "coming soon" tile, must not soften live verdicts structurally (hedged voice is the thing the verdict exists to not be — the margin lives in claim choice, not in mush), must not automate verdict text, and must not read a long dark period as license to lower a gate — the gate defines when there is enough season to argue about, and the argument waits for it.

**Amendment (2026-07-23, season-over-season design — ADR-0060/0061).** The flip moves a pointer; it no longer replaces an argument. With per-season arguments (ADR-0060), "the season a hero page renders" means the canonical season behind the `/<slug>` alias: the flip PR moves `canonicalSeason`, and the prior season argument survives at its own season permalink, verdict frozen verbatim, gaining only the structural forward link to the current season. When the hero has a prior argued season, the flip PR may also author the first growth-sentence under the growth claim kind (ADR-0061) — optional, like every vocabulary family. Everything else above stands: the gates trigger, a human authors, the season runs dark until they pass.
