# Jargon terms are click-opened dictionary popovers

Product jargon — shot diet, Making Δ, PPS, expected points per shot, catch-and-shoot vs pull-up, FGA, Share — is defined in place by a dictionary popover: the term renders as a real button (dotted underline, its sentence's own type; `Term`, `src/app/Term.tsx`), and click/tap opens a small definition card. One interaction model on every device, ADR-0027's rule extended from zone details to vocabulary: hover is a visual affordance only, never the opener. Definitions live in one registry (`src/app/glossary.ts`) that re-presents CONTEXT.md's Language entries in general-reader words.

**Why:** the page argues to an executive/general reader, and the words carrying the argument are the words that reader doesn't know — the copy can't inline-define "shot diet" at every use without burying the argument, and a glossary section would put the definition a scroll away from the confusion. Hover tooltips and `title=` attributes are invisible to touch (ADR-0018/0027 said it for zone details; the same reasoning binds here). A popover puts two sentences exactly where the reader stumbled, on every device.

**Consequences:** the glossary is structural product copy, identical for every hero — never a per-hero claim (so no verdict-guard obligations) and never a per-hero number; league-general arithmetic (the PPS example) is allowed, the "highest-value shot" band-note precedent. Definitions speak the product's vocabulary as CONTEXT.md defines it — a definition never introduces a synonym the glossary avoids. The card portals to `document.body` and positions `fixed`, so the tables' `.zone-scroll` overflow containers can never clip it; it clamps inside the viewport, flips above the trigger when the space below runs out, and repositions on any scroll or resize while open. The trigger is a real button (`aria-expanded`, `aria-haspopup="dialog"`) whose text is exactly the copy it wraps — table header assertions on `textContent` hold unchanged, but `getByText` on a wrapped sentence must switch to a `textContent` check (the button splits the text nodes). The card follows ZoneDetailCard's dialog contract: `role="dialog"`, focused on open so Escape works immediately, dismissed by close button, Escape, or an outside press; focus returns to the term on Escape/close-button dismissals and stays put on outside presses; not a focus trap, not `aria-modal`. One card at a time falls out of outside-press dismissal — no coordinator. Terms wrap structural copy only — section descriptions, headline subtitles, table column headers, creation row labels — never the authored verdict (auto-linking hero prose is future work, and `screen.getByText(hero.verdict)` stays exact) and never `aria-label` strings. **Prose wraps a term once, at its first reading-order mention** (amendment, same-day feedback): repeat wraps read as hand-holding — "expected points per shot" is a term in the selection card only, "shot diet" in the headline subtitle only. Table column headers are the exception and stay uniformly wrapped per table: a table is a reference surface a reader can land in cold, and per-table uniformity beats global dedupe. Triggers must typeset EXACTLY like the copy they wrap — `font`/`letter-spacing`/`text-transform` all explicitly `inherit`, because Chrome's UA button stylesheet resets `text-transform: none` and silently un-capitalized the uppercase table headers. Adding a term is one registry entry plus wrap sites.
