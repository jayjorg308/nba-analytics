# The creation payload is a parallel contract with its own league baseline and cross-payload reconciliation

v2.0's data arrives pre-aggregated (the NBA tracking dashboards have no per-shot rows), so creation ships as a **second typed payload**, parallel to the derived payload and never joined to it: a separate deployed file per hero-season (`public/data/<slug>/<season>.creation.json`), its own Zod schema with its own `SCHEMA_VERSION` (starting at 1), its own golden fixture, metric-free per ADR-0007's rule. Two context families ship: **General** (catch-and-shoot / pull-up / less than 10 ft / other) and **Shot Clock**; closest-defender is a deferred fast-follow (a routine schema bump); dribbles and touch-time are rejected as restating General. The **league creation baseline is not free** the way v1's was — it is a separate league-wide pull, rolled up by summing FGM/FGA/FG2M/FG3M per context across the league (the ADR-0004 rule, never averaging rates), and persisted inside the creation payload exactly as `zoneBaseline` rides inside the shot payload. The creation payload is **required** for every registered hero.

**Why separate, not folded in:** the two contracts evolve on different clocks — the defender-distance follow-up bumps only the creation schema while the shot golden stays byte-stable — and folding a sibling domain into the locked ADR-0007 seam re-opens a shipped contract for no data reason. Why a real league baseline: every diet claim in the product is "vs league average, stated plainly" (ADR-0002); a creation diet without a comparison class can state a share but cannot make an argument. Why required: an optional payload forks the product into two classes of hero page (some with a why-sentence, some without) and plants a permanent conditional in the page; both current heroes get spike pulls anyway, and a hero re-add just includes one extra local pull. The eligibility cost is stated as a third gate — **the tracking gate**: tracking data exists for the season (2013-14 onward) — the ADR-0003 pattern extended.

**Reconciliation (ADR-0004 extended cross-payload):** the General family must partition the season exactly — **Σ context FGA = the shot payload's `totalShots` + `zoneConflictsDropped`** (the pre-drop season total: the dashboards know nothing of our ADR-0019 drops) — asserted at derive time, hard-fail, loud (the ADR-0028 ethos; a creation payload contradicting its sibling is never persisted). Shot Clock is held to **coverage, not equality**: tracking gaps leave attempts unattributed, so the derive computes the shortfall, persists it in `_meta`, and the UI reports it whenever nonzero (the ADR-0019 exclude-and-report pattern — NBA-side gaps must not veto the payload, and must not hide either). Both identities are additionally guarded by a pytest reconciliation test over the goldens and by the skippable real-data vitest over the **deployed** pair, so a one-sided `hero:sync` cannot quietly ship contradictory payloads.

**Player-side metrics:** a second pure function, `aggregateCreationMetrics`, beside `aggregateShotMetrics` — each with its single production call site (ADR-0009/0011 extended to a sibling domain, not violated: the rule guards against a second site computing the *same* quantities). Creation PPS is computed in TS from each context's 2PT/3PT makes — PPS, never eFG%, one value unit across the product (ADR-0001).

**Closed from the spike (2026-07-15 — `ingestion/pull_tracking.py`, both heroes' 2025-26 + league):**

- **The General identity holds exactly.** Cody: Σ 509 = 509 totalShots + 0 dropped; Keyonte: Σ 881 = 880 + 1 dropped — the pre-drop identity behaves as designed (the ADR-0019 drop shows up on cue), so the hard-fail guard ships as specified.
- **The shot-clock coverage gap is zero in launch data** — both heroes and league-wide (Σ six bands = Overall FGA; no 'ShotClock Off' band exists). The coverage posture stays: the reported count reads 0 until some season proves otherwise.
- **Context literals, and two traps catalogued.** Player rows: `'Catch and Shoot'` / `'Pull Ups'` / `'Less than 10 ft'` / `'Other'` (`SHOT_TYPE`); `'24-22'` / `'22-18 Very Early'` / `'18-15 Early'` / `'15-7 Average'` / `'7-4 Late'` / `'4-0 Very Late'` (`SHOT_CLOCK_RANGE`). Trap one: the row set is **sparse** — a zero-attempt context is an *absent row* (Cody has no `'Other'` row), so the derive fills zeros and the schema expects every context. Trap two: the league filter literals **differ in case** from the row literals (`'Pull Ups'` → `'Pullups'`, `'Less than 10 ft'` → `'Less Than 10 ft'`; recorded per snapshot in `_meta.resolved_filters`). League `'Other'` is not filterable and is computed as the **residual by count subtraction** (Overall − Σ resolved) — counts, never rates, so the ADR-0004 rule is preserved in reverse.
- **Shot Clock consolidates in the aggregation, not the payload.** The payload carries the NBA's six-band grain verbatim; `aggregateCreationMetrics` rolls up to three product bands — **Early (24–15) / Average (15–7) / Late (7–0)** — summing makes and attempts on both the player and baseline sides (the ADR-0016 combined-threes pattern: fine grain persisted, verdict grain computed). On real counts every product band clears the flag for both heroes (Cody 195/243/71; Keyonte 349/377/155), so the clock story carries no † at product grain.
- **The shared <50 † constant fits.** It flags exactly the thin rows (both heroes' `'24-22'`, Cody's two late bands, Keyonte's 2-attempt `'Other'`) and nothing else; no creation-specific constant is needed (ADR-0031 stands).
- **League mechanics.** Per-context filtered `leaguedashteamptshot` calls (30 team rows each; `per_mode_simple='Totals'` is the one flag that matters — the endpoint defaults to per-game averages, which would silently corrupt the reconciliation and every baseline), summed at derive per ADR-0004; totals are coherent (General resolved + residual = Overall; Σ Shot Clock = Overall). For the defender fast-follow: the result sets are named `ClosestDefenderShooting` / `ClosestDefender10ftPlusShooting`.

**v2.1 (2026-07-16) — the deferred family shipped.** Closest Defender landed as creation **schema v2**, exactly the routine bump this ADR anticipated: schema, derive, regenerated golden, and re-synced deployed payloads moved together. Facts from the pull: the family **partitions the season exactly** for both heroes and league-wide (zero unattributed everywhere — the coverage counters exist and read 0), and the league filter literals match the row literals (no case traps this time). Product grain, the clock-band pattern: **Tight 0–4 ft** ('Very Tight' absorbed — alone it sits sub-bar at 44/48 attempts) / **Open 4–6** / **Wide open 6+**, every band clear of the flag for both heroes. The 10-ft-plus defender variant stays raw-only — a future jumpers×defender cross would be a new decision. The payoff row sharpened the launch argument with independent tracking data: 0.880 PPS on 117 wide-open attempts against the league's 1.178, while his tight and open bands sit at league — the value leaks precisely where nobody is guarding him.
